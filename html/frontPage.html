Popular Recipes

Tinolang Manok

A comforting chicken soup with ginger, green papaya, and malunggay leaves, perfect for chilly days.

Tinolang Manok
:
Ingredients:

1. 1 whole chicken, cut into serving pieces
2. 1 thumb-sized ginger, sliced
3. 1 onion, chopped
4. 2 cloves garlic, minced
5. 1 tbsp. fish sauce
6. 1.5 liters water
7. 1 small green papaya, peeled, seeded, and cut into wedges
8. 1 bunch malunggay leaves
9. Salt and pepper to taste

Procedure:

1. In a pot, ginger, onion, and garlic until fragrant.
2. Add chicken and cook until slightly browned.
3. Pour in water and bring to a boil.
4. Simmer until chicken is tender.
5. Add green papaya, and malunggay leaves.
6. Season with salt and pepper.
7. Serve hot and enjoy!

Adobong Manok

A savory dish made with chicken stewed in soy sauce, vinegar, garlic and pepper

Adobong Manok
Ingredients:

1. 1 kg chicken or pork, cut into pieces
2. 1/2 cup soy sauce
3. 1/2 cup vinegar
4. 1 head garlic, minced
5. 1 onion, chopped
6. 2 bay leaves
7. 1 tsp. peppercorns
8. 1 cup water
9. Salt and pepper to taste

Procedure:

1. Marinate meat in soy sauce, vinegar, and garlic for at least 30 minutes.
2. In a pot, sauté onion and garlic until fragrant.
3. Add marinated meat and cook until browned.
4. Pour in marinade, add bay leaves and peppercorns.
5. Add water and bring to a boil.
6. Simmer until meat is tender and sauce is reduced.
7. Season with salt and pepper.
8. Serve hot and enjoy!

Kare-Kare

A peanut-based stew with oxtail, tripe, and vegetables, often paired with bagoong (fermented shrimp paste).

Kare-Kare
Ingredients:

1. 1 kg oxtail, cut into pieces
2. 1/2 kg tripe, cleaned and cut into pieces
3. 1 bunch sitaw (string beans), cut into 2-inch pieces
4. 1 banana heart, sliced
5. 1 eggplant, sliced
6. 1/2 cup peanut butter
7. 1/4 cup ground toasted rice
8. 1 onion, chopped
9. 2 cloves garlic, minced
10. 1 tbsp. annatto powder
11. Salt and pepper to taste

Procedure:

1. Boil oxtail and tripe until tender.
2. In a pot, sauté onion and garlic until fragrant.
3. Add annatto powder and mix well.
4. Add boiled oxtail and tripe, and cook for a few minutes.
5. Pour in peanut butter and ground toasted rice, and mix well.
6. Add vegetables and cook until tender.
7. Season with salt and pepper.
8. Serve hot with bagoong (shrimp paste) and enjoy!

Halo-Halo

A colorful dessert made of crushed ice, milk, sweetened beans, fruits, jelly, and topped with leche flan or ube ice cream.

Halo-Halo
Ingredients:

1. 1 cup crushed ice
2. 1/2 cup evaporated milk
3. 1/4 cup sweetened beans
4. 1/4 cup sweetened fruits
5. 1/4 cup jellies
6. 1 scoop ice cream
7. Leche flan (optional)
8. Ube halaya (optional)

Procedure:

1. In a tall glass, layer sweetened beans, fruits, and jellies.
2. Add crushed ice on top.
3. Pour evaporated milk over the ice.
4. Top with a scoop of ice cream, leche flan, and ube halaya if desired.
5. Serve immediately and enjoy!

Pancit

Stir-fried noodles with vegetables, meat, or seafood, often served during celebrations as a symbol of longevity and prosperity.

Pancit
Ingredients:

1. 1 pack pancit noodles
2. 1/2 kg chicken or pork, sliced
3. 1 carrot, julienned
4. 1/2 cabbage, shredded
5. 1 onion, chopped
6. 2 cloves garlic, minced
7. 1/4 cup soy sauce
8. 1/4 cup oyster sauce
9. 1 cup chicken broth
10. Salt and pepper to taste

Procedure:

1. In a pan, sauté onion and garlic until fragrant.
2. Add chicken or pork and cook until browned.
3. Add soy sauce, oyster sauce, and chicken broth, and bring to a boil.
4. Add noodles and cook until tender.
5. Add vegetables and cook until tender.
6. Season with salt and pepper.
7. Serve hot and enjoy!

Sinigang na Baboy

A sour pork soup with vegetables like kangkong, radish, and eggplant, loved for its tangy flavor.

Sinigang na Baboy
Ingredients:

1. 1 kg pork
2. 1 packet tamarind soup base
3. 1 onion, chopped
4. 2 tomatoes, quartered
5. 1 radish, sliced
6. 1 bunch kangkong (water spinach)
7. 1 eggplant, sliced
8. 1 liter water
9. Salt and pepper to taste

Procedure:

1. In a pot, onion and tomatoes until soft.
2. Add meat and cook until slightly browned.
3. Pour in water and bring to a boil.
4. Add tamarind soup base and mix well.
5. Add radish and eggplant, cook until tender.
6. Add kangkong and cook for another 2 minutes.
7. Season with salt and pepper.
8. Serve hot and enjoy!
